# Ecommerce Profitability & Sales Intelligence Dashboard

## Client Background & Business Problem

The client owns an e-commerce store that generates consistent monthly sales. However, they face a critical challenge:

"I feel like I'm driving blindfolded. Sales are coming in, but I have no idea where the profits are going."

The available data was massive and scattered: sales transactions, competitor prices, shipping costs, product ratings, seasons, and holidays. Despite having all this data, the client lacked clarity on:

- Which products generate real profit after deducting shipping costs?

- Are the highest-priced products actually the most profitable?

- Where are profits being silently "leaked"?

The client needed to transform raw numbers into clear, actionable business decisions.

## 🎯 Project Objective

The goal of the project was to design an interactive Power BI dashboard that answers two critical business questions:

### 1. What is the overall performance of the ecommerce store at a glance?

An executive summary page was created to provide quick visibility into:

- Total Sales
- Net Profit
- Shipping Costs
- Profit Margins
- Monthly Sales Trends
- Category Performance
### 2. Which products are truly profitable after shipping costs?

A profitability analysis page was designed to identify:

- High-profit products
- Low-margin categories
- Shipping cost inefficiencies
- Products contributing most to overall profitability

## Key Insights

- Revenue Concentration: Top 3 categories (Health/Beauty, Watches, Garden) drive 60% of total revenue, demanding focused investment and protection.

- Margin Erosion: Garden Tools shows highest shipping cost ratio (2.8%) due to heavy product weights, reducing its 97.2% margin versus the 98.6% average.

- Extreme Polarization: Health 2 alone achieves a near-perfect 99–100% margin with 212K in sales, while console games suffered a 94% margin collapse, exposing a high-risk profitability gap.

- Growth Trajectory: Monthly sales multiplied 8× from January 2017 to December 2018, with peak season concentrated in May–November 2018, signaling strong — but cyclical — business expansion.

## Strategic Recommendations

- Reduce dependency on low-margin gaming products
- Expand focus on high-margin health products
- Optimize shipping cost strategy for low-profit categories
- Increase marketing campaigns during seasonal peak periods
- Monitor category-level profitability continuously through dashboard KPIs
